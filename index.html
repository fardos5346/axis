The X and Y Axes

Score: 0 / 20

Why do we need it?

The X and Y axes create a coordinate system that allows us to pinpoint any exact location on a flat surface using just two numbers. Without it, describing positions in maps, graphics, and data visualization would be incredibly difficult.

Understanding the 4 Quadrants

Quadrant 2 (Q2)
(- , +)
Negative X / Positive Y
Quadrant 1 (Q1)
(+ , +)
All Positive
Quadrant 3 (Q3)
(- , -)
All Negative Mix
Quadrant 4 (Q4)
(+ , -)
Positive X / Negative Y

Why do we learn it?

Learning coordinates is the foundation for algebra, geometry, and data science. It helps us understand graphs, design video games (positioning objects on a screen), navigate using GPS, and read maps efficiently.

A Little History

This system is known as the Cartesian coordinate system, named after the French philosopher and mathematician René Descartes, who introduced it in the 17th century. Legend says he came up with the idea while watching a fly crawl across his ceiling, realizing he could describe its position using distances from the walls.
